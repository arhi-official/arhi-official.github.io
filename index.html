ARHI: Human-Guided Reward Adaptation for Real-Robot Reinforcement Learning

ARHI Framework Overview. ARHI addresses the misalignment of pretrained rewards by employing a dual-loop online adaptation framework that integrates value relabeling and human action overrides, achieving superior sample efficiency and robustness in real-world tasks.

Abstract

Real-world reinforcement learning enables robots to adapt directly to complex physical dynamics, avoiding the reality gap inherent in simulation and rendering previously infeasible manipulation skills a reality. However, the high cost of real-world exploration strictly limits sample availability. While sparse rewards offer stability, maximizing sample efficiency typically necessitates dense supervision extracted from pretrained models. The challenge, however, is that these generic priors are often misaligned with the specific distributions encountered during real-world deployment.

In response, we propose ARHI, a parameter-efficient online reward adaptation framework that adapts such dense and faithful guidance in real time. To achieve this, we employ a decoupled fine-tuning strategy that anchors generalizable semantic knowledge within a frozen backbone, while channeling adaptation exclusively through a lightweight head to dynamically calibrate the reward model against on-policy dynamics. Integrated into an asynchronous dual-loop framework, this continuously updating reward drives policy optimization, ensuring precise alignment to accelerate sample-efficient convergence. Extensive real-world experiments on six manipulation tasks demonstrate that ARHI outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, improving task success by 23.3% and reducing human effort by 28.0%, while achieving a 47.6% reduction in performance drop under challenging deployment shifts.
